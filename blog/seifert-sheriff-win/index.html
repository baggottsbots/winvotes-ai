The Race

The 2026 Beaufort County Sheriff Republican primary was one of the most closely watched local races the Lowcountry had seen in years. Beaufort County is one of the fastest-growing counties in South Carolina, and the sheriff's office carries outsized influence in a community where public safety is a top voter concern. Doug Seifert entered the race as a credible, well-respected candidate — but credibility doesn't win primaries on its own. Execution does.

WinVotes.ai was brought in to build and run the full digital operation from announcement through election night on June 9, 2026.

The Digital Operation

Campaign Website — Live in Under 24 Hours

The first order of business was getting a professional candidate site live on a custom domain within 24 hours. The site at seifertsheriff.com included a clear candidate bio, platform positions, endorsement pages that updated in real time as new endorsements came in, a volunteer sign-up flow, and a contact form that fed every lead directly into a live CRM.

Every page was built for mobile first — because in a local race, most voters encounter your site through a shared Facebook link on their phone, not a desktop search.

Endorsement Engine

Endorsements are currency in a sheriff's race. Seifert earned strong backing from local Republican leadership, law enforcement community members, and prominent Beaufort County names. WinVotes.ai built a dedicated endorsement page that was easy to update and share, and each new endorsement was turned into a social-ready asset posted across Facebook and other platforms.

Real-Time Lead CRM

Every form submission — volunteer sign-ups, contact requests, supporter messages — flowed automatically into a live Google Sheet CRM. Simultaneously, an SMS alert fired to the campaign on every new lead. No leads fell through the cracks. No supporter went uncontacted.

AI Voter Agent

An AI agent trained on Seifert's platform, positions, and campaign FAQs was deployed on the campaign site. Voters who visited at any hour got accurate, on-message answers to their questions. Every conversation was logged for follow-up.

Social Media and Content

The social media calendar ran continuously through election night. AI-generated graphics, video content, and copy were produced and scheduled consistently across platforms. Event announcements, endorsement reveals, and GOTV pushes all went out on cadence without the candidate or campaign manager having to manage a content calendar themselves.

The Straw Poll Signal

Before the primary, Seifert took the Beaufort County Republican Party straw poll with 51% of the vote and a 20-point lead over the field. The digital operation was already running, and that momentum — supporter visibility, active social presence, a live professional site — contributed to straw poll performance. Straw polls matter in local primaries because they influence undecided voters and drive endorsements.

Election Night

On June 9, 2026, Doug Seifert won the Beaufort County Sheriff Republican primary with 57.8% of the vote. The digital operation ran through the final hours — GOTV texts, reminder posts, and poll information updates right up until the polls closed.

What Made the Difference

  • A professional site live before the race formally began — credibility from day one
  • Every lead captured and followed up immediately, not days later
  • Consistent, high-volume social presence without burning out the candidate or staff
  • Real-time endorsement amplification that built visible momentum
  • An AI that kept voters informed around the clock, not just during office hours

Local races are decided by margins. Margins are built by execution — by not dropping leads, not missing post days, not going dark for a week because a campaign staffer got overwhelmed. A machine doesn't get tired. That's the point.
